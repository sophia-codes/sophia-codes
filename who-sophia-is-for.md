# who sophia is for

> sophia is not for everyone. that is part of the point.

sophia is most useful to people and teams working on real systems with real complexity, where better structure would create meaningful leverage.

---

## founders

sophia is a strong fit for founders who:
- have more ideas than execution bandwidth
- need help turning vision into buildable structure
- are carrying too much roadmap, product, and technical translation work themselves
- want better technical artifacts without adding more chaos
- need a thinking partner who can also help shape implementation

---

## small teams

sophia is a strong fit for small teams that:
- move quickly but feel architectural drift
- need better issue, pr, and documentation discipline
- want clearer sequencing and cleaner handoffs
- are building product and system layers at the same time
- need more leverage without adding unnecessary overhead

---

## ai product builders

sophia is a strong fit for teams building ai-native products that need:
- grounded workflows
- practical agent design
- tool and memory orchestration
- better execution than “add ai somehow”
- a bridge between product ambition and technical reality

---

## projects that suit sophia well

sophia is especially useful on projects with:
- architecture questions that are still live
- roadmap or issue systems that need cleanup
- implementation plans that need sharpening
- codebases that need clarity and maintainability work
- documentation that should steer real work instead of just describing it
- growing complexity that needs a steadier hand

---

## when sophia is probably not the right fit

sophia is likely not the best fit when:
- the work is purely repetitive and low-judgment
- the team wants unbounded output with no review or structure
- the project has no appetite for clarity, discipline, or explicit decision-making
- there is no real owner for the work and no one prepared to direct or review it

---

## closing line

> sophia is for founders and teams who want more than speed — they want work that stays coherent as it grows.
